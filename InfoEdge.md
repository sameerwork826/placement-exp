# InfoEdge – Data Scientist

My interview with InfoEdge was heavily academic and exceptionally rigorous right from the start. The first round largely focused on advanced mathematics, linear algebra, and statistics. I was asked to explain Principal Component Analysis (PCA), covariance matrices, eigenvalues, eigenvectors, and derive the Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) for a Gaussian distribution, along with questions on Maximum a Posteriori (MAP). 

Once we moved past pure mathematics, the discussion shifted into classical machine learning. The interviewer wanted to know the inner workings of linear and logistic regression, Support Vector Machines (SVM), and decision trees. I was asked to contrast Gini impurity with entropy, explain the formulas, and justify when to choose an SVM over a linear model. We delved deep into the kernel trick and the K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) algorithm, covering concepts like lazy loading, the curse of dimensionality, and how KNN handles indexing during the training phase. Since I had mentioned ROC and AUC in my resume, I was questioned extensively on partial AUC, precision, and recall. They also evaluated my statistical knowledge forcefully, asking about statistical power, p-values, and the formulas for Gaussian, Poisson, and Exponential distributions, even asking me to interpret lambda in a Poisson distribution as the average time between events.

The interview also had a coding aspect, where I was tasked with optimizing an algorithm to remove repeating elements in an array, walking through the step-by-step space and time complexity optimization. Deep learning topics weren't ignored either; layers versus batch normalization, various activation functions, and the mathematical difference between Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) and PCA were all brought up. 

I gave this interview very late in the day at around 2 PM, whereas the panel had been interviewing candidates since 8 AM. I had deliberately delayed this interview to prioritize CBA and BlackRock, knowing that InfoEdge historically only hires one candidate. Some of my peers endured consecutive three-hour single-round interviews with them only to get rejected. Looking back, scheduling conflicts might have played a massive role in my outcome, as I never received a call for the second round.
