# CBA (Commonwealth Bank of Australia) – AI Engineer / Data Scientist

This was the role I was primarily targeting and had prepared extensively for.

In the first round, I was asked questions on Git/GitHub, DSA (sorting and DP), and my projects. There was a deep discussion on vector databases like ChromaDB, similarity search, and HNSW indexing. I was also asked SQL questions orally and core Python OOP concepts like abstraction and encapsulation.

The second round was more of an ML system design discussion based on my background. I was asked how I would help improve my family’s school business. I proposed multiple ideas including AI-based marketing, building an application for school operations, and integrating an LLM chatbot for student-related queries. There was also an in-depth discussion on my loan risk prediction internship, particularly around the use of LLMs and handling PII data. The interviewer also discussed how their organization uses in-house models instead of APIs, which led to a broader discussion about model deployment and company strategies.

The third round was an HR and culture fit round. I was asked behavioral questions about achievements, failures, and situations involving conflicts with seniors. I mostly answered using my POR experiences, although at one point I was asked to shift focus to academic examples. The round went on for quite long and overall felt very positive.

Despite performing well across all rounds and being confident about my chances, I was not selected. This was a major learning experience about the unpredictability of final decisions.
