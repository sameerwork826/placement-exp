# TCG Digital – Consultant

I was initially shortlisted for the Consultant role after passing a test that included an aptitude section and two interesting system design essays. One essay prompted me to design a machine learning system utilizing cameras for a chemical factory, and the other asked more philosophical aspects of AI, like whether it has attained consciousness.

The first interview round was incredibly rushed and impromptu. An HR representative called me exactly as I was waiting for another major interview with LEK Consulting to begin. Despite my protests to delay, he insisted it would only take fifteen minutes, so I opted to take it immediately. The panel consisted of an HR member, a technical specialist, and a product manager. It was a pure resume-grilling session. We jumped straight into my machine learning internship, focusing intensely on gradient boosting techniques like XGBoost. We then transitioned into a credit card fraud detection project I had listed. They pushed me on my specific choices for dataset features, my logic for selecting a particular evaluation metric to maximize, and the overall trade-offs of optimizing for recall versus precision or F1-scores. 

The second round involved the HR and product managers probing further into other projects on my resume. We discussed my marketing internship in detail, analyzing how I utilized Google Analytics to drive strategies for a local coaching institute. The topic then shifted to a Generative AI project I built, which was a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline designed to fetch company details. The technical questions were highly specific: why I chose LLaMA 7B, how I selected ChromaDB as my vector database, what parameters dictated my embedding model selection, and the nuances of the chunking hyperparameters I implemented. 

I successfully answered the vast majority of their questions and felt very good about the technical deep dive. However, because I simultaneously received and accepted an offer from LEK Consulting right afterward, the recruitment process effectively halted, and I never heard back regarding the final outcome.
