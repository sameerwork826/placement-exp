# LEK Consulting – Analyst (Selected)

This was the most chaotic yet rewarding experience of my placement journey due to overlapping interviews and connectivity issues, but I somehow managed to complete all rounds.

In the first round, I was given a case study on product cannibalization. The task was to analyze whether launching a new product would increase overall profits or lead to losses. The evaluation was based on both quantitative and qualitative reasoning, and the approach mattered a lot.

The second round involved two panel members and started with HR questions, followed by a case study on a merger and acquisition scenario. I was asked what metrics and factors I would consider while analyzing the acquisition, including identifying red flags and growth opportunities.

The third round was conducted by a senior manager and was more of a fit check. I was asked a guesstimate question on the number of traffic signals in a city and some general HR questions about my understanding of the company and my future plans.

I was selected for the role. The position is more of a backend consulting role where I will work with global consulting teams rather than directly with clients, but it offers strong exposure to the consulting domain.
