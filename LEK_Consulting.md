# LEK Consulting – Analyst (Selected)

My experience with LEK Consulting was one of the most chaotic yet rewarding parts of my placement journey. I was the very first candidate scheduled for an interview, but severe connectivity issues caused the meeting to disconnect early on. At the exact same time, a recruiter from TCG Digital was urgently calling me for their first technical round. I ended up managing a frantic juggling act: holding off LEK by convincing my placement representative to intervene, completing the fifteen-minute TCG interview, and then seamlessly jumping back into the LEK process to somehow complete all three of their rounds successfully.

The first round was heavily focused on a business case study. I was presented with a scenario concerning product cannibalization, where a company wanted to launch a new product and needed to evaluate whether this would increase overall profits or inadvertently cannibalize their existing revenue streams. I had to evaluate the quantitative and qualitative metrics provided by the interviewer, explicitly pinpointing how the revenue numbers could shift. Out of the hundred students shortlisted, the emphasis here was heavily placed on having an structured, analytical approach rather than precisely guessing the final numbers. 

The second round was conducted by a panel of two members—an HR representative and a senior manager. After about thirty minutes of standard behavioral questions, they hit me with a mergers and acquisitions case study. The prompt revolved around a prominent South Indian Quick Service Restaurant chain looking to acquire another business. They provided various metrics, such as a localized subset of hero products with 30-40% CAGR, alongside stagnant menu items. I was tasked with analyzing these metrics, identifying potential green and red flags, and concluding whether the acquisition was a sound strategy. 

The third round was led by a senior team manager from the LEK LCN office. It was a general fit-check round. He began by asking a guesstimate question regarding the number of traffic signals in a specific city. After I broke down my logic and arrived at a number, he provided the actual data for Delhi as a sanity check, which aligned closely with my approach. We then discussed my aspirations for an MBA, my willingness to relocate, and my overall understanding of LEK Consulting. 

While the role is for an analyst at a top-tier consulting firm rather than a purely technical Data Science position, I've always viewed myself as a blend of a product and data professional. I see immense value in the exposure to the consulting industry, particularly as a foundational step toward a future MBA. It’s worth noting that this is a backend consulting role—meaning I'll be supporting LEK’s global consultants directly behind the scenes rather than acting as a client-facing representative. Ultimately, I was one of the three students from my campus selected for this prestigious role.
